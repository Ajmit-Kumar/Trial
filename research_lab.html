Research Lab

Sustainable Energy Research Laboratory (SERL): This lab is being developed with a target of undertaking research in renewable energy and two-phase flow. Focused areas are two-phase flows in microchannels for developing sustainable cooling solutions to electronic devices, analysis of nuclear clad materials, solar thermal technology etc.

The lab is involved with ongoing research on two-phase flow boiling in mini and microchannels, pool boiling, electrowetting, passive heat transfer enhancement techniques etc. In addition, research works have been also performed in analysis of flow and heat transfer in non-Newtonian fluids.

Research works undertaken in SERL are jointly initiated by Prof. Manabendra Pathak and Dr. Mohd. Kaleem Khan

Attached Labs: Fluid Mechanics, Heat and Mass Transfer, Sustainable Energy Research Laboratory (SERL)

First two labs are basically for undergraduate students performing experiments on fluid mechanics, fluid machineries and heat transfer.
