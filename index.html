Manabendra Pathak

Professor

Indian Institute of Technology Patna

I am a Professor in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Indian Institute of Technology Patna, India. My research and teaching focuses on the various aspects of Mechanical/Aerospace Domain.

My main research project, A self-adaptive electronic cooling system by enhanced pool boiling, focuses on the performance enhancement of proposed cooling system and is supported by Science and Engineering Research Board, DST, Govt. of India.

Interests
  • Computational Fluid Dynamics and Heat Transfer
  • Turbulence Modeling
  • Two-phase flow in micro and minichannels
  • Dispersion of particles, droplets and bubbles at micro- and nano-scales
  • Rheological and heat transfer characteristics of viscoplastic fluids
  • Nuclear materials
  • Solar thermal technology

Biography

I joined IIT Patna as an Assistant Professor in March 2009. Prior to joining IIT Patna, I worked as a post-doctoral fellow in the Department of Chemical Engineering, Technion Israel Institute of Technology from July 2007 to February, 2009. I had also experience of working as a staff member in the Department of Mechanical Engineering, IIT Guwahati and as a design engineer at M.T.I. (Pvt) Ltd. I completed B.E. (Mechanical) from Assam Engineering College, M.Tech. (fluid/thermal) and Ph.D. (Mechanical) from Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati.
